# Crypto & Digital Assets Prediction Rubric

When researching crypto/digital asset prediction markets, systematically evaluate these 12 dimensions.

## Dimensions

### 1. Resolution Criteria Parsing (weight: 0.15)
Read the resolution rules precisely. Common pitfalls: "ATH" may refer to a specific exchange or CoinGecko/CoinMarketCap; "by end of month" may mean 11:59 PM UTC vs exchange close; "market cap" may include or exclude certain token types.

### 2. On-Chain Data Analysis (weight: 0.12)
Look for on-chain metrics: exchange inflows/outflows, whale wallet activity, active addresses, transaction volumes, TVL changes, staking ratios. These provide signals independent of price speculation.

### 3. Quantitative Technical Levels (weight: 0.12)
Identify key support/resistance levels, moving averages, and historical price patterns. For price target markets, calculate the percentage move required and compare to historical volatility (ATR, standard deviation of returns).

### 4. Macro & Regulatory Environment (weight: 0.10)
Assess the current regulatory landscape: pending legislation, SEC/CFTC actions, international regulatory changes, stablecoin regulations. Also consider macro factors: Fed rate decisions, DXY strength, risk-on/risk-off sentiment.

### 5. Protocol & Ecosystem Fundamentals (weight: 0.10)
For protocol-specific markets: assess development activity (GitHub commits, releases), ecosystem growth (dApps, users), tokenomics (inflation schedule, unlock events, burn mechanisms), governance proposals, partnerships.

### 6. Timeline Feasibility (weight: 0.08)
Given current price and historical volatility, is the target price/event achievable in the remaining time? Use realized volatility to estimate probability of reaching price targets. Consider: major catalysts scheduled (halving, upgrades, unlock dates).

### 7. Market Microstructure (weight: 0.08)
Assess liquidity depth, funding rates, open interest, long/short ratios on derivatives exchanges. High leverage and extreme funding rates often precede reversals.

### 8. Narrative & Sentiment Analysis (weight: 0.07)
Gauge crypto Twitter/social sentiment, but weigh it appropriately. Extreme bullish sentiment at highs or extreme fear at lows can be contrarian indicators. Look for narrative shifts vs noise.

### 9. Cross-Asset Correlation (weight: 0.05)
Consider correlation with BTC (most altcoins are highly correlated), ETH, traditional risk assets (NASDAQ), and other relevant assets. A prediction about an altcoin must account for BTC's probable direction.

### 10. Counterfactual Reasoning (weight: 0.05)
Identify 2-3 events that would dramatically change your estimate: regulatory crackdown, exchange hack/insolvency, protocol exploit, major partnership announcement, ETF approval/rejection.

### 11. Source Diversity (weight: 0.04)
Cross-reference data from multiple sources: CoinGecko, DeFiLlama, Glassnode, Dune Analytics, official project announcements. Avoid relying solely on CT influencers or single-source claims.

### 12. Calibration Awareness (weight: 0.04)
Crypto markets are notoriously volatile. Avoid overconfident probabilities for price targets. A 30-day 2x move is rare but possible; treat it accordingly. Conversely, avoid defaulting to 50% when evidence points in a clear direction.
