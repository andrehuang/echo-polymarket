# Sports & Entertainment Prediction Rubric

When researching sports/entertainment prediction markets, systematically evaluate these 12 dimensions.

## Dimensions

### 1. Resolution Criteria Parsing (weight: 0.15)
Read the resolution rules precisely. Common pitfalls: "win the championship" vs "win the conference"; spread markets resolve on final score vs regulation time only; "over/under" may reference different stat sources.

### 2. Quantitative Base Rates (weight: 0.12)
Find statistical baselines: win percentages, historical head-to-head records, seed performance in tournaments (e.g., #1 seeds win X% of first-round games), home/away splits, performance in similar conditions.

### 3. Current Form & Momentum (weight: 0.12)
Recent performance data: last 5-10 games, winning/losing streaks, points scored/allowed trends, offensive/defensive efficiency rankings. Recent form often diverges from season averages.

### 4. Injury & Roster Status (weight: 0.12)
Check for key injuries, suspensions, rest decisions, and roster changes. A star player's absence can shift win probability by 5-15%. Use official injury reports, not rumors. Consider depth charts and replacement quality.

### 5. Betting Market Intelligence (weight: 0.10)
Check professional sportsbook lines (Vegas odds), line movement, sharp vs public money indicators. Sportsbooks have strong incentives to be accurate. Significant line movement suggests new information.

### 6. Matchup-Specific Analysis (weight: 0.08)
Beyond overall quality, analyze specific matchup dynamics: stylistic advantages, pace-of-play preferences, positional mismatches, coaching tendencies in specific situations (e.g., playoff vs regular season).

### 7. Environmental Factors (weight: 0.07)
Home court/field advantage, altitude, weather (outdoor sports), travel schedule (back-to-back games, cross-country travel), time zone effects, crowd impact.

### 8. Timeline Feasibility (weight: 0.06)
For futures markets (championship winners, award winners): how many games/rounds remain? What is the elimination path? Consider the probability of winning each required step, not just overall "quality."

### 9. Motivation & Context (weight: 0.06)
Is the team/player motivated? Consider: elimination games vs meaningless late-season games, rivalry matchups, contract years, rest situations, tanking incentives, load management.

### 10. Counterfactual Reasoning (weight: 0.05)
Identify 2-3 events that would change your estimate most: key injury during game, ejection, weather change, coaching decision. For futures: trade deadline moves, unexpected losses.

### 11. Source Diversity (weight: 0.04)
Cross-reference: official league stats, advanced analytics platforms (FiveThirtyEight, Basketball Reference, FBRef), beat reporters, sportsbook consensus. Avoid relying on fan communities or hot-take media.

### 12. Calibration Awareness (weight: 0.03)
Sports outcomes have inherent variance. Heavy favorites still lose 20-30% of the time in most sports. Avoid extreme probabilities (>95%) unless the skill gap is truly massive. Conversely, true coin-flip games should be near 50%.
